I am a 5th year Dual Degree (BTech+MTech) student at Computer Science and Engineering Department, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur.

I have worked at the Computer Science and Automation (CSA) Department in Indian Institute of Sciences (IISc) Bangalore as a summer and winter research intern in 2019, funded by the Narendra Summer Internship Scholarship. There I worked with Prof. Arindam Khan on 2 Dimensional Guillotine Knapsack. The problem lies in the confluence of Computational Geometry and Approximation Algorithms. The work got published at 37th International Symposium on Computational Geometry ( SoCG'21).

At IIT Kharagpur, as a part of my undergraduate research, I have worked essentially on Algorithmic Game Theory and Parameterized Algorithms advised by Prof. Palash Dey. Notable works include: Liquid Democracy (published at 7th Annual International Conference on Algorithms and Discrete Applied Mathematics (CALDAM'21) ), Margin Of Victory for Tournaments and Network Design for Binary Public Goods Games.

Research

My prior research work revolves around the design and analysis of Approximation and Parameterized Algorithms for Computational Geometric and Game Theoretic Problems.

On Guillotine Separable Packings for the Two-dimensional Geometric Knapsack Problem
Arindam Khan, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma and Andreas Wiese
37th International Symposium on Computational Geometry
SOCG 2021
Full Version

A PPTAS (Pseudo-Polynomial Time Approximation Scheme) for 2 Dimensional Knapsack problem in a Guillotine Separable arrangement improves the previous best approximation factor of 3.

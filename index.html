I am a 5th year Dual Degree (BTech+MTech) student at Computer Science and Engineering Department, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur.

I have worked at the Computer Science and Automation (CSA) Department in Indian Institute of Sciences (IISc) Bangalore as a summer and winter research intern in 2019, funded by the Narendra Summer Internship Scholarship. There I worked with Prof. Arindam Khan on 2 Dimensional Guillotine Knapsack. The problem lies in the confluence of Computational Geometry and Approximation Algorithms. The work got published at 37th International Symposium on Computational Geometry ( SoCG'21).

At IIT Kharagpur, as a part of my undergraduate research, I have worked essentially on Algorithmic Game Theory and Parameterized Algorithms advised by Prof. Palash Dey. Notable works include: Liquid Democracy (published at 7th Annual International Conference on Algorithms and Discrete Applied Mathematics (CALDAM'21) ), Margin Of Victory for Tournaments and Network Design for Binary Public Goods Games.
I have been taking tutorials [1, 2] in Algorithmic Game Theory Course (CS60025) as a Teaching Assistant offered by Prof. Palash Dey (CSE, IIT Kharagpur) in Autumn 2021. As a tutor, the clarity of this beautiful subject magnifies each time I lecture and solve problems (Magically, I can solve even those problems which I was stuck as a student in the same course in 2019.

Research & Publications

I'm interested in Theoretical Computer Science, including Approximation, Online, Randomized, Parameterized Algorithms, Algorithmic Game Theory. My prior research work revolves around the design and analysis of Approximation and Parameterized Algorithms for Computational Geometric and Game Theoretic Problems.

Here is the list of research projects completed by me with publications in highlighted in yellow. (Note: For theory papers, author names are listed alphabetically.)

On Guillotine Separable Packings for the 2D Geometric Knapsack Problem
Arindam Khan, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma and Andreas Wiese
SOCG 2021
Contributed Talk HALG 2021
[SoCG Version] [arXiv] SoCG Presentation | HALG Presentation | Poster

A PPTAS (Pseudo-Polynomial Time Approximation Scheme) for 2 Dimensional Knapsack problem in a Guillotine Separable arrangement improves the previous best approximation factor of 3.
Two Dimensional Guillotine Strip Packing
Arindam Khan, Aditya Lonkar, Arnab Maiti, Amatya Sharma and Andreas Wiese
Submitting to ICALP 2022 [arXiv]

(3⁄2 + ε)-Polynomial time approximation algorithm for the 2 Dimensional Strip problem in a Guillotine Separable arrangement, complementing the lower bound of (3⁄2 + o(ε)). Also, formulated PPTAS (Pseudo-Polynomial Time Approximation Scheme) for the same.

On Parameterized Complexity of Liquid Democracy
Palash Dey, Arnab Maiti and Amatya Sharma,
7th Annual International Conference on Algorithms and Discrete Applied Mathematics
CALDAM 2021
Full Version | Conference Version | Video

Design and Analysis of Parameterized Algorithms for the problem of Liquid Democracy that is a democracy with voters allowed to delegate their votes to others.
Online Randomized Weighted k-server problem
Nikhil Ayyadevara, Ashish Chiplunkar, and Amatya Sharma,

Formulation of online randomized algorithm for a variant of weighted k-server problem, mitigating the gap between established upper bound and lower bound complexities
Parameterized Complexity of Nash Equilibrium of Networked Public Good Games
Palash Dey, Amatya Sharma,

Algorithmic analysis of PSNE for Game Theoretic Problem of Networked Public Good Games. Established parameterized hardness and formulated XP-algorithms.
The Art Gallery Problem : A Survey
Dewang Modi, Adarsh Singh Parihar and Amatya Sharma,
Submitted to ACM Computing Surveys Journal

Long Survey paper peruse NP-hardness, ∃R − Completeness, bounds and algorithms in approximation and parameterized complexity for the classic Art Gallery Problem and its variants.

Parameterized Complexity of Margin of Victory
Palash Dey and Amatya Sharma,

Developed Parameterized Algorithms for Game-Theoretic problem of computing Margin of Victory for tournament solutions
